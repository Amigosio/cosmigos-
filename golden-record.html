// Earth's Electromagnetic Hum — Schumann Resonance

7.83 Hz
FUNDAMENTAL FREQUENCY — THE HEARTBEAT OF EARTH

What Is It?

The Schumann resonance is an electromagnetic frequency that exists between Earth's surface and the ionosphere. Lightning strikes (50-100 per second globally) keep this cavity resonating. It's the electromagnetic heartbeat of Earth itself.

Why Is It On The Record?

Carl Sagan included Earth's electromagnetic signature as a kind of fingerprint — proof of our planet's existence. Any civilization advanced enough to find Voyager could use it to locate Earth by its unique electromagnetic profile.

The 7.83 Hz Mystery

This frequency matches the alpha brainwave state of a relaxed human mind (8-12 Hz). Some researchers speculate life evolved in sync with this frequency. Whether cosmic accident or adaptation, we literally resonate with our planet.

Changing Over Time

The Schumann resonance has been increasing slightly since the 1950s — from 7.83 Hz to occasional spikes of 8.5 Hz. Climate change and ionospheric shifts are altering Earth's electromagnetic voice. We're changing our own frequency.

Harmonics

Beyond 7.83 Hz, the resonance has harmonics at 14.3, 20.8, 27.3 and 33.8 Hz. These overtones create a complex chord — Earth's ambient hum is actually music, just too slow for most instruments.

If Aliens Hear It

The inclusion of Earth's EM signature is arguably the most dangerous thing on the record. It's a beacon. Carl Sagan knew this. He decided to say hello anyway. That is either very brave or very reckless. Possibly both.
