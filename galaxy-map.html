Laniakea Supercluster

520 MILLION LY ACROSS

Home sweet home. Contains ~100,000 galaxies including our Milky Way. Discovered in 2014 — we didn't even know our own address until recently.

YOUR SUPERCLUSTER

Perseus–Pisces Filament

300 MILLION LY LONG

One of the largest structures visible from Earth. A massive cosmic wall of galaxy clusters, stretching across the sky like a cosmic Great Wall.

NEARBY NEIGHBOR

Coma Supercluster

300+ MILLION LY ACROSS

Home to the famous Coma Cluster with over 1,000 galaxies. Dark matter was first proposed here — scientists noticed galaxies orbiting too fast.

DARK MATTER BIRTHPLACE

Shapley Supercluster

650 MILLION LY ACROSS

The most massive concentration of galaxies in the local universe. Contains tens of thousands of galaxies and is actually pulling our supercluster toward it.

GREAT ATTRACTOR REGION

Boötes Void

250 MILLION LY WIDE EMPTINESS

A massive cosmic void — almost completely empty. If the Milky Way were inside it, we wouldn't have discovered other galaxies until the 1980s.

THE GREAT NOTHING

Hercules–Corona Borealis Wall

10 BILLION LY ACROSS

The largest known structure in the observable universe. So large it challenges the cosmological principle — that the universe looks the same in all directions.
