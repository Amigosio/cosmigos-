The bright side of the accretion disk appears brighter due to Doppler boosting — matter rushing toward you glows hotter.
Time near the event horizon runs slower. One hour at the photon sphere ≈ thousands of years on Earth.
The first black hole image (M87*) was taken in 2019. It took years of computation and a planet-sized telescope.
